Design Probes

Background

This project was done as part of a module focused on participatory design. The goal of the project was to take an area of life and develop a participatory design activity in order to learn more about it. I decided to look at the future of autonomous cars as my area of life. I was interested in the future of autonomous cars given that the way cars are designed today prioritises the act of driving. Autonomous cars open up an opportunity to totally rethink car design. Given the option of Design Games, Critical Design and Design Probes, I chose to use design probes. I felt that Design Probes would be a great way to identify interesting insights in this area.

Once completed, I recruited participants and provided them with my design probes. I took their responses and reflected upon them, drawing key insights from what they had provided. I then designed something in response to what I’d drawn from the feedback.


What are Design Probes

Design probes are a form of participatory design where participants are provided with items that prompt them to provide information about their lifestyle and feelings. They are an indirect and sometimes provocative way for learning about a group of people and can uncover insights that designers often don’t think to ask about in a traditional interview. They are often seen as a good starting point for opening up a dialog with participants.


What I developed

The probes I developed consisted of 6 task sheets and an introduction sheet. I created these in Adobe Illustrator and wanted to make them colourful, animated and engaging. I had learned that the more effort that is put into making the probes generally results in more effort in response to them. The tasks were split up in two sessions. I provided three task sheets to participants in week 1 and 3 tasks sheets to participants in week 2 in order to lighten the load. I hoped that participants would be less inclined to rush the probes if there were less to get through.

The design probes resulted in some very interesting insights about travel and some direction for autonomous car design. I was most interested by the idea of cars being associated with road trips which was highlighted by some participants. I decided to prototype an app for an autonomous car but focused specifically on developing a trips feature which would allow users to create and share memories. The autonomous car could be used to take photos and using AI could automatically contribute to trips if enabled by the user.


Introduction Probe


The introduction probe was intended to thank participants for their time as well as to provide some context about the exercise they were about to take part in. It let them know how they were meant to work through the probes. It was also intended to prompt the participant to think freely, be open minded and to be comfortable with any response they provide. In some cases the more obscure responses are the most insightful.

Probe 1


Probe 1 was aimed at gathering some direction in terms of how an autonomous car interior should be designed. The participants were asked to draw the inside of their ideal cabin which they would be spending a significant amount of time and travelling great distances in. I refrained from letting the participants know that this was a car design as I was concerned that preconceived ideas of how car interiors should be might influence the participants response. In my prior research I noticed that many speculative autonomous car concepts were somewhat similar to conventional car interior design and wanted to get away from this.

Probe 2


Probe 2 was created in response to prior research which highlighted a sense of discomfort that passengers can have with autonomous vehicles. I wanted to try and find out what makes passengers feel safe on different forms of transport.

Probe 3


For Probe 3 I wanted to learn about the form of interaction that a user might have with their autonomous car and the kind of information that might be presented to them. I suggested to the participants that their car has started its own website and asked them to list the kinds of things they could use the website for. I hoped that this would invoke some more creative responses.

Probe 4


Probe 4 revisited the task in probe 1. This would be carried out in the following week. The participant is asked to redesign their cabin from probe 1 taking into account that they will now be sharing the cabin some days of the week. I thought that this would highlight the important aspects of their design and reemphasise what they value.





Probe 5


Probe 5 was intended to highlight the aspects of luxury travel that participants would most highly value. The participant is told that they’ve been upgraded to first class and asked what aspects of their flight they’re most looking forward to.

Probe 6

Probe 6 asks participants to share photos of the things that they always want to have with them when they travel. I asked them to exclude phone wallet and keys as I felt these would be obvious answers.

App


Following on from the probe participation and assessment of the responses I designed an app which would be used to control an autonomous vehicle. I specifically focused on a trips feature which would allow users to create memories which consist of photos and videos from journeys they’d been on. One of the challenges of this app was the amount of information and tasks that it would need to manage. I decided to split the app into a profile and car section.
